The Science Behind It
WHY IT
WORKS.

Crazy 8 works not despite its constraints — because of them. Time pressure, quantity requirements, and analog tools trigger distinct cognitive states that digital brainstorming alone cannot replicate.

01 Divergent Thinking
QUANTITY FORCES QUALITY

The first seven rounds push you into divergent mode — generating ideas without judgment. Sixty seconds is too short to second-guess yourself. You sketch and move on before your inner critic can speak.

Over eight rounds you're pushed past the safe first answer, past the variation-of-the-first-idea, and into territory you wouldn't reach by thinking alone. Research consistently shows the most original ideas emerge in later cycles — after the obvious has been exhausted. Crazy 8 guarantees you get there.

02 Convergent Thinking
SELECTING AND COMBINING

Round 8 shifts your mind toward synthesis. After generating at speed, the brain craves evaluation. The completion prompts aren't decoration — they're a deliberate phase change built into the structure.

  • Which idea surprises you? Surprise signals genuine novelty — unexpected ideas are always worth a closer look.
  • Which solves the problem best? Returns you to the original brief after a session of unconstrained thinking.
  • Can two ideas be combined? Hybrid ideas frequently outperform either source. This is where breakthroughs tend to live.

The gap between making and evaluating is critical. Crazy 8 builds that gap into its structure — you cannot skip from generation directly to execution.

03 Paper & Pencil
LOW FIDELITY IS THE FEATURE

A pencil sketch made in 60 seconds cannot be mistaken for a finished product. That roughness is not a limitation — it is the entire point.

Low fidelity = low attachment

When you've spent 60 seconds on a drawing, you can discard it without hesitation. When you've spent three hours on a digital mockup, you can't. Paper removes the sunk-cost trap from the process entirely.

Sketches invite collaboration

A rough box with an arrow communicates "there's a button here" without accidentally fixing the visual design. Polished mockups shut down feedback. Rough sketches open it up and invite challenge.

Inclusive by default

Anyone can participate regardless of software skills. A pen is the universal design tool. The playing field is completely flat — no Figma licence, no learning curve, no barrier.

Research supports it

Studies on design fixation (Jansson & Smith, 1991) show high-fidelity references early in the process constrain output — designers copy features rather than explore alternatives. Research in embodied cognition shows the physical act of drawing activates spatial reasoning and motor memory in ways digital tools do not replicate.

About this project

Crazy 8 is a timed ideation tool for product designers, UX researchers, and teams who need to generate eight distinct ideas in eight focused minutes. It uses voice coaching, visual progress tracking, and structured reflection prompts to move from blank page to convergent thinking in one session.
